## Pre-Retrospective Preparation

---

## 1️⃣ Phase Overview (Facts Only)

Write down:

* Levels you were involved in (Level 1 / Level 2)
* Sessions you attended or led
* Average attendance you noticed
* Sessions where attendance noticeably dropped

---

## 2️⃣ What Went Well (Be Specific)

List **3–5 concrete points**:

* Sessions or topics that worked well
* Teaching methods that students responded to
* Tasks or activities that were effective
* Any positive student feedback you heard

✍️ Example:

> Session 3 on APIs had strong engagement and many questions.

---

## 3️⃣ What Didn’t Go Well (Honest & Specific)

List **3–5 problems** you observed:

* Weak sessions or topics
* Content that was too fast or too heavy
* Student confusion points
* Instructor or preparation issues

✍️ Example:

> Git was rushed and students struggled during tasks.

---

## 4️⃣ Learning Outcomes Reality Check

For each skill below, write what you think students **actually** reached:

* Programming fundamentals
* Git & GitHub
* Databases
* APIs / backend logic
* Debugging & problem solving

Use:

* ✔️ Good
* ⚠️ Partial
* ❌ Weak

Add **why** if possible.

---

## 5️⃣ Engagement & Commitment Observations

Answer briefly:

* When did students start losing interest?
* Common excuses for absence?
* Difference between Level 1 and Level 2 motivation?
* Did tasks affect attendance positively or negatively?

---

## 6️⃣ Team & Process Feedback

From your perspective:

* Were instructors well prepared?
* Was communication clear?
* Any overload on specific people?
* Documentation issues?

---

## 7️⃣ Suggestions for Next Phase

List **at least 2 suggestions**:

* Curriculum changes
* Pace adjustments
* Engagement ideas
* Structural improvements

Focus on **realistic actions**.

---

## 8️⃣ One Hard Truth 🧠

Write **one uncomfortable but important truth** we should face as a committee.

✍️ Example:

> We assumed students knew basics they didn’t actually know.

---

## 📌 Important Notes

* No blaming, only improvement.
* Bring written notes (bullet points are enough).
* We’ll prioritize facts over opinions during discussion.
